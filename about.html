About Anime

Anime is a style of animation from Japan known for its colorful visuals, unique characters, and deep storytelling. It covers many genres like action, romance, fantasy, and more — for all age groups.

Quick Fact about First Anime

  • Title: Namakura Gatana
  • Creator: Jun'ichi Kōuchi
  • Released: 1917
  • Location: Japan
  • Style: Silent, traditional frame-by-frame hand drawing

Who Introduced Animation?

Animation as an art form was developed by several pioneers over time. Here are the most important names in animation history:

Émile Cohl – "The Father of Animation"

French cartoonist and filmmaker
Created the first fully animated film: "Fantasmagorie" (1908) – a hand-drawn stick figure cartoon.
Considered the first true animator in history.

Winsor McCay – American Animation Pioneer

American cartoonist and animator
Famous for "Gertie the Dinosaur" (1914) – one of the first animated characters to show personality.
His work helped establish animation as a storytelling medium.

Walt Disney – The Man Who Popularized Animation

Creator of Mickey Mouse and founder of The Walt Disney Company.
Released the first full-length animated movie, "Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs" (1937).
Made animation a global industry and art form.
